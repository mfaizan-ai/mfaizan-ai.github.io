I am a PhD candidate in Psychology at [Trinity College Dublin](https://www.tcd.ie), working in the [Cusack Lab](https://www.cusacklab.org/home.html) under the supervision of [Prof. Rhodri Cusack](https://www.tcd.ie/research/profiles/?profile=cusackrh). My research aims to develop physics-informed neural network frameworks that integrate domain knowledge of MRI acquisition with data-driven learning. Specifically, I investigate methods for multiband fMRI reconstruction, particularly in infant imaging, where motion presents a significant challenge, and I aim to jointly estimate and correct motion directly in k-space, where the raw signal contains richer and less-processed information compared to the image domain. By operating in k-space, the goal is to more accurately model motion-induced artifacts and improve the fidelity of reconstructed fMRI data. Before beginning my PhD, I completed my Master’s degree in Robotics and Intelligent Machine Engineering at the [National University of Sciences and Technology](https://nust.edu.pk) (NUST), Islamabad, Pakistan. My Master’s research focused on developing a hybrid deep neural network architecture for brain tumor segmentation using multi-modal structural brain MRI. This work was supervised by [Dr. Sara Baber Sial](https://research.monash.edu/en/persons/sara-sial/).
